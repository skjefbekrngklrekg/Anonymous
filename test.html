ControlFlow: Streaming Action Prediction via Dynamic Magnitude Scaling

This page provides supplementary material for anonymous review. We present synchronized qualitative comparisons between Streaming Flow Policy and the proposed ControlFlow, initialized from the same starting state. All demonstrations use state-based observations as input and are executed under an online streaming inference setting with a maximum rollout horizon of 200 steps.

Streaming Flow Policy (Baseline)

Baseline behavior under a fixed action magnitude. Using state-based inputs, the policy performs online inference with conservative action updates. Within the 200-step rollout horizon, the baseline may require substantially more steps to approach the target and, in some cases, may not complete the task.

ControlFlow (Proposed)

ControlFlow with dynamic magnitude scaling. Under the same state-based input and maximum 200-step inference horizon, the proposed method adapts action magnitude online during streaming inference, where λ serves as a modulation variable that controls the effective action scale. This adaptive scaling enables faster task completion while avoiding excessive actions that could lead to over-pushing.
